Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) has become a popular term over the last couple of decades. Instead of only focussing on general intelligence, some experts beelive that being able to understand and express emotions can play an equal part in how someone will do in life

How is EQ different to IQ?

IQ - cognitive intelligence/book smarts

A number that comes from a standardised IQ test that is calculated by comparing the test taker’s score to the average scores of other people in the same age group. The IQ represents:

  • Visual processing
  • Working memory
  • Knowledge of the world
  • Fluid reasoning
  • Quantitative reasoning
EQ - emotional intelligence/street smarts

This refers to a person’s ability to perceive, control, evaluate, and express emotions. EQ is centred on abilities such as:

  • Identifying emotions
  • Relating to others
  • Perceiving how others feel
  • Using emotions to facilitate social communication
  • Controlling one’s own emotions

Why is it important?

EQ is important because it is now believed to be an integral part of a person and even desirable to hiring managers when lookinf for a new employee. Employers are now looking at candidates’ EQ by doing EQ tests to gauge how they can fit within the organisation. Both EQ and IQ contribute to overall success. Perhaps the greatest benefit may lie in improving one’s cognitive skills along with social and emotional skills.
