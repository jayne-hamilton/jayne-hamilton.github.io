Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity, what is it?

Neuroplasticity is the ability of the brain to modify its connections or re-wire itself. There are plenty of benefits such as:

  • The ability to learn new things
  • The ability to enhance existing cognitive capabilities
  • Recovery from strokes and traumatic brain injuries
  • Strengthening areas where function is lost or has declined
  • Improvements that can boost brain fitness

Neuroplasticity is responsible for learning, developing, and forming memories. The brain is consistently rewiring itself and modifying its connections.

Knowledge is key, and in this case the knowledge of neuroplasticity means unlocking so much potential to learn and grow.There are ways to increase neuroplasticity such as learning a new language, reading and diet.

Growth Mindset

When you look up “Growth Mindset”, the internet tells us that Growth mindset describes a way of viewing challenges and setbacks. People who have a growth mindset believe that even if they struggle with certain skills, their abilities aren't set in stone. They think that with work, their skills can improve over time.

A growth mindset is looking at problems as interesting challenges that need to be fixed. I found that the more that I explored the growth mindset the more I believed in how vital it is for me on my learning journey. Having gone for so many years with the thoughts of learning a complete new skillset was too hard and that I couldn’t possibly understand meant that I started to really believe it.

I have integrated a growth mindset into my own learning journey by taking the time to study each day. Before I start studying I take time each morning to be present and mindful of where I am and set intentions for the day. Part of this routine is to acknowledge that through this learning journey I am facing new concepts for the first time which can seem daunting, I like to acknowledge this at the start of the day so I am aware and present that with a growth mindset I can do this.
