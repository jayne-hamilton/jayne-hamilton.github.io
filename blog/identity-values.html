Exploring My Core Values & Identity

While thinking about my own values, I asked myself 3 questions
  1. "What are my core values?"
  2. "Where have I learnt these values?"
  3. "Why are these values so important to me?"

To answer these questions, I thought about my childhood. I have very fond memories growing up. As a kid, I always felt that I was safe and loved but like many kiwi families of the time, we didn’t openly discuss our core values as a family much.

Looking back, I had to move away from home and travel the world to really learn my own identity and values. I’m grateful to the twenty-one-year-old me for heading overseas on a whim and experiencing an array of different cultures. I didn’t understand how important it was at the time but backpacking had a positive impact on me. Travelling alone encouraged me to meet new people and experience new situations and over time I discovered what traits I value as important. My personal identity and beleifs have been formed over time from my experiences but honesty, empathy and creativity have been consistent on my journey.

The phrase "you are who you surround yourself with" really resonates with me, I’ve realised that surrounding myself with like-minded people is important to me. Spending time with people who want to see me achieve my goals and celebrate my wins is important to me. I feel like I can be a better version of myself when I am surrounded by people who I want to be like.

Thoughts on Strengths and Limitations

When considering what I felt my strengths and limitations were, I wondered if the people closest to me would agree with how I viewed myself. I took the High5test to see what it suggested my top 5 strengths were. The results were surprisingly what family and friends (and past colleagues!) have described me as with focussed being number one.

Being focused and organised can mean efficiency in the workplace but it can also be a downfall if you end up spending too much time trying to plan every single thing to go a certain way. I feel as though setting intentions and goals each day/week is helpful as long as I am taking the time to reflect and to be present when I need to be.

Summary of strengths:

  1. Focus Expert

    2. your objective is to focus on what’s most important

  2. Deliverer

    3. your underlying goal is to take responsibility

  3. Philomath

    4. your objective is to learn new things

  4. Timekeeper

    5. your objective is to set timelines and deadlines

  5. Empathizer

    6. your objective is to be empathetic to others’ emotions

I've found that sometimes limitations mean reaching out for help from others or perhaps taking time to upskill in order to complete a task. There have also been times when limitations can occur from others while working in a team. I find that when resistance or tension occurs, I try to understand that each person is coming from a different set of values and experiences and I try to see it from their point of view. Usually, if I approach a tense situation with that mindset then I'll remain calm and we can reach an agreement.
